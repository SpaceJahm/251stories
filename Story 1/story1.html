The Giving Tree

Once there was a Tree

and she loved everyone very much

And everyday they all would come

and they would all gather the tree's leaves

And make them into crowns and played together

They would climb up the Tree's trunk

and swing from the Tree's branches

and eat the Tree's apples

And they would play hide and seek

And when they were tired they went home and slept comfortably in their own bed

And they all loved that tree

very much and the tree was happy

But time went by

And everybody grew up

and the tree was left alone

Then one day some of the everybody came to the tree and the tree said : “ Come, everyone, come and climb up my trunk and swing from my branches and eat apples and play in my shade and be happy.”

We are too big to climb and play,” said everyone. “ We want to buy things and have fun. We want some money. Can you help us earn my own money?

And so everyone climbed up the tree and gathered the tree's apples and carried them away. And the tree was happy . . .

But them everyone stayed away for a long time . . . and the tree was sad. And then one day everyone came back and the tree shock with joy and she said, “ Come, everyone, climb up my trunk and swing from my branches and be happy.”

"We are too busy to climb trees,” everybody said. “ We want a house to keep us warm,” everyone said. “ I want to have a life, and so I need a house. Can you give me a house?

“ I have no house,” said the tree. “ The forest is my house, but you may cut off my branches and build a house. Then you will be happy.”

And so everyone shared the trees branches and carried them away to build their house's.

And the tree was happy

But once again, everyone stayed away for a long time. And when they came back, the tree was so happy, the tree could hardly speak. “ Come” she whispered, “ come and play.” “ I am too old and sad to play,” said everyone. “ We want a boat that will Take me far away from here. Can you give us a boat?” “ Cut down my trunk and make a boat,” said the tree. “ Than you can sail away… and be happy.”

And so everyone cut down and shared the trunk and made a boat and sailed away.

And the tree was happy . . . but not really

And after a long time everyone came back again. “ I am sorry,” said the tree, “ but I have nothing left to give you, my apples are gone.” “ My teeth are too weak for apples,” said everyone. “ My branches are gone,” said the tree. “ You cannot swing on them” “ We are too old to swing on branches,” everyone said. “ My trunk is gone,” said the tree. “ You cannot climb” “ We are to tired to climb" everyone said

“ I am sorry,” sighed the tree. “ I wish that I could give you something… But have nothing left. I am just an old stump. I am sorry…” “ We don’t need very much now,” “ just a quiet place to sit and rest. I am very tired.” “ Well,” said the tree, straightening herself up as she could, “ well, an old stump is good for sitting and resting. Come, sit down. sit down and rest.” And everyone did.

and the tree was happy

THE END
